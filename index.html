DVD RENTAL ANALYSIS

In this project, I executed a variety of SQL queries to extract, analyze, and manipulate data from multiple relational databases. The queries involved retrieving specific records, filtering and aggregating data, creating and joining tables, and generating insights such as customer spending patterns, film ratings, and transactional data summaries.

In this project, I implemented complex SQL operations to manage and analyze large datasets. I utilized SELECT queries with filtering conditions such as WHERE, LIKE, and BETWEEN to extract targeted information from customer and payment data. Advanced functions like SUM(), COUNT(), MIN(), and AVG() were employed to perform aggregate calculations, identifying trends in customer spending and transaction volumes. To enhance the structure and usability of data, I created new tables and inserted records using the CREATE and INSERT commands. Additionally, I used INNER JOIN and LEFT JOIN to merge data across multiple tables, facilitating the analysis of relationships between films, inventory, and payments. Sorting and limiting results were handled with ORDER BY and LIMIT, while grouping was achieved using GROUP BY and HAVING to identify customers who met specific conditions. This comprehensive approach allowed for an in-depth analysis of key business metrics and insights into customer behavior.
